requirements for my dataset:
domain alignment: questions must fall into the knowledge scope of the unarXive 2024 Dataset that SQuAI uses
    -lot of datasets target biomedical (verify!)
    -unArxive targets physics, math, cs, quantitative biology (verify)    

complex questions: 
    - SQuAI aims to answer complex scientific questions, a good dataset should reflect that, ideally questions are obtained by experts, but that’s too expensive for a bachelor's thesis
    - decomposeablity?
multi document reasoning questions 
    - (because of 4 agent pipeline, question decomposition), multi-hop

CONTEXT INDEPENDENCE / open-domain questions: questions must contain all context to be standalone answerable
    -this includes not:
    -referencing the paper or its content the questions is originally based on
    -example “What is the significance of the parameter λ in the context of this paper?”
    -referencing concepts which are not uniquely identifiable without the general context of the paper
    -example: “What is the Island Concept” (references a well known concept that loses its identifiability without knowing which general topic is talked about)

text format: 
    - currently SQuAI solely relies on information in the full text of papers without any data from graphs/ images, thus questions may only target those knowledge

ground truth: 
    - needed to evaluate if the model answers correctly

ExpertQA
    domain alignment: strong match if sorted out to the fields that squai also has
    complex questions: strong match, challending, require reasoning and open-ended
    multi document reasoning questions : yes, but sources are weblinks, not scientific papers
    context independence: stron match, questions are context independent
    text format: yes
    ground truth: ground truths are given, but they are weblinks not scientific papers --> hard evaluation or filtering to squai dataset
    --> only 2177 examples over 32 fields --> way to small if filtered to unArxive information (todo make educated guess how many left after filtering here)
    --> GOLD standard but unsuitable
notes:
    -expert curated questions, then ask expert to evaluate responses to their own questions and improve upon those responses (expert in the loop evaluation) --> tries to simulate information seeking scenario with experts
    -field relevant questions as well as claim level judgement
    -verifyability is important for high stake fields (e. g. medicine and law)
    - questions are collected by askins experts what they are curious about or what they have encountered --> no concrete fact basis
    - questions answered by LLM based systems with attributions (purely generative, retrieval augmented, post hoc)
    - answer and evidence then judged by expoert for informativeness, citeworthiness and factuality (and how faithfull claim is to accompanying evidence and how reliable the evidence's source is) 
    - finally experts revise each claim, so it is faithful to reliable evidences and factually correct
    - 1. Retrieve-and-read systems generate more complete attributions compared to LLM prompting and post-hoc attribution, but struggle to produce citations for all cite-worthy claims.
    - 2. The retrieval source significantly impacts the quality of attribution and overall factuality.
    3. High-stakes domains such as medicine and law suffer from a large percentage of incomplete attributions (35% and 31% incomplete attributions respectively) and many attributions come from unreliable sources (51% attributions are not rated reliable by experts).
    - also measures that automtic methods for attribution and factuality fall short to the experts evaluation afterwards
