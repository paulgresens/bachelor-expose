    for 8 and 9:
    For instance, LaQuer maps generated text to source spans by analyzing internal cross-attention weights to see where the model was "looking." Similarly, gradient-based attribution methods like GRAINS utilize Integrated Gradients to calculate the causal influence of specific input tokens, identifying exactly which parts of the context drove the model's output. While highly precise for token-level attribution and internal steering, these methods require full open-source access to the underlying model architecture, hidden layers, and gradients, rendering them incompatible with proprietary LLMs. To maintain flexibility and model-agnosticism, this thesis focuses entirely on black-box, retrieval-based extraction pipelines.
